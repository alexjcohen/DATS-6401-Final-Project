Demographic Indicators - Education

Demographic indicators such as education may also be likely to influence the average housing and rent prices. The more "educated" the city (measured by completion of high school or attainment of a bachelor's degree), the likely higher wages that individual may be able to command and thus, the more they may be able to pay towards their living expenses. Let's see if this holds true.

It appears as though high-school graduation rates has little to no impact on the average housing and rent prices between 2010 and 2017. It also appears as though the rate of college completion (bachelor's degree attainment) has little relationship to the median home price.

However, it appears as though the rates of bachelor's degree completion may be slightly correlated with the median rent price, potentially suggesting that as more people finish college and look towards apartments upon completion of school (rather than houses), rent prices are able to increase accordingly.
