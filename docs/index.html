Introduction

Housing and rent prices have been increasing all over the country in the past decade post economic collapse of 2008, with a significant increase evident in some of the largest metropolitan areas in the US. This project aims to explore the key factors that may be driving this resurgance in housing and rent prices. This analysis will focus on the years 2010 - 2017.

The data for housing and rent prices comes courtesy of zillow.com, the population data comes directly from the US Census Bureau at the state level, and the indicator data comes courtesy of the US Census Bureau's American FactFinder website, with the specific economic, age, income, and education statistics found using the following search terms:

  • Age and Sex: S0101
  • Education: S1501
  • Employment: S2301
  • Income: S1903

Additionally, each of the 15 cities examined in the following analysis has been categorized by region according to the distribution below:
